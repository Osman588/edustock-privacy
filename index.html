Privacy Policy

Effective date: May 17, 2026  ·  Last updated: May 2026
This policy applies to the EduStock mobile application for Android.

1. Who We Are

EduStock is an independent educational application built for students in Grades 6 to 12. We are not affiliated with, endorsed by, or connected to any government body, provincial textbook board, or ministry of education. The app is developed and operated by an individual developer based in Pakistan.

When this policy says "we", "us", or "our", it refers to the developer of EduStock. When it says "you", it refers to anyone who uses the app.

2. What Information We Collect and Why

2.1 Information You Give Us Directly

Name and email address — collected when you create an account. Stored in Google Firebase. Used to identify your account and restore your progress across devices.

Phone number — collected only if you choose phone-based login. Used by Firebase for verification. Not stored separately by us.

Profile picture — uploaded optionally by you. Stored on Google Firebase Storage. Displayed only to you inside the app.

Password — never stored by us. Managed entirely by Google Firebase Authentication using their security infrastructure.

2.2 Information Generated by Your Use of the App

Learning progress — quiz results, badges earned, topics viewed. Stored in your Firestore account document. Used to power the streak system and badge awards.

Last open date and daily streak count — recorded each time you open the app. Stored in your Firestore account document. Used only for the learning streak feature.

Language preference and theme preference — saved to your Firestore document so your settings persist across devices.

Highlights and underlines on notes — saved locally on your device only using SharedPreferences. This data never leaves your device and is never uploaded to any server.

FCM token — a device-specific token generated by Firebase Cloud Messaging. Stored in your Firestore document. Used only to deliver push notifications from the app to your device. It does not identify you personally.

Search history (local cache) — recent search terms are saved locally on your device using SharedPreferences for faster repeat searches. Never uploaded to any server.

2.3 Information Collected Automatically by Third-Party SDKs

The app uses Google services that collect certain data automatically. We do not control this collection — it is described in Google's own privacy policies linked in Section 5.

Firebase Crashlytics — if the app crashes, it sends a crash report to Google containing the crash location in code, device model, Android version, and app version. No personal information is included in crash reports.

Google AdMob — serves advertisements in the app for users who are not on ad-free accounts. AdMob collects your device's Google Advertising ID (a resettable identifier), device model, Android version, and approximate location derived from your IP address. This is used to decide which ads to show you. It is handled entirely by Google's systems.

Firebase App Check — verifies that requests to our backend come from the genuine EduStock app, not from automated scripts. Does not collect personal information.

2.4 What We Do Not Collect

We do not collect your precise GPS location, contacts, call logs, camera data, microphone input, health information, financial information, or any data not described above.

4. Administrator Access

The app developer, acting as the administrator, can see the name, email address, account creation date, badge count, streak, language setting, and ad-free status of registered users. This access exists through the Firebase Console and the in-app admin panel.

This access is used only for: managing ad-free access grants, responding to support requests, and monitoring app stability. It is not shared with any other person or organisation.

The administrator may also collect your name and email address directly within the app in connection with specific in-app features (such as granting ad-free access or enabling beta features). You will be informed at the time of any such collection what the data is used for.

6. Advertisements

EduStock is free to use and is supported by advertisements delivered by Google AdMob. Ads are shown only to users whose accounts have ads enabled. The app administrator can grant individual users an ad-free experience, in which case no advertisements are shown to that user.

We do not control which specific advertisements Google shows. The content of advertisements is determined entirely by Google's systems based on factors including your Advertising ID. To opt out of personalised ads on your Android device, go to: Settings → Google → Ads → Opt out of Ads Personalisation. You can also reset your Advertising ID from the same menu.

Admin accounts never see advertisements regardless of settings.

7. Children

EduStock is intended for students aged 13 and above. We do not knowingly collect personal data from children under the age of 13. If a parent or guardian becomes aware that their child under 13 has created an account in the app, please contact us at the address in Section 11 and we will delete the account and its associated data promptly.

We have not configured AdMob to serve child-directed ads. The app is rated for users aged 13 and above.

8. Data Sharing

We do not sell your data. We do not share your data with advertisers, marketing companies, data brokers, or any organisation outside of the Google services listed in Section 5.

Your data may be disclosed if required by applicable law or a valid legal order. In that case, we would disclose only what is specifically required.

9. Data Retention

Your account data (name, email, progress, preferences, badges) is retained in Firestore for as long as your account exists. When you delete your account, your Firestore document is marked as deleted and your Firebase Authentication account is removed.

Crash reports collected by Firebase Crashlytics are retained for 90 days by default, after which Google deletes them automatically.

Local data (highlights, search cache) is stored only on your device and is removed when you uninstall the app or clear the app's storage.

AdMob data is retained according to Google's own retention policies, which we do not control.

10. Deleting Your Account and Data

You can permanently delete your account and all associated Firestore data at any time by going to Settings → Delete Account inside the app. You will be asked to confirm your password before deletion. This action cannot be undone.

For data held by Google's systems (Crashlytics, AdMob), you can manage it through Google's account controls at myaccount.google.com.

11. Security

All communication between the app and Firebase servers is encrypted using HTTPS. Passwords are never stored by us — they are managed by Firebase Authentication. Firebase Security Rules restrict access so that each user can only read and write their own data. OTP codes used for two-factor authentication are stored as cryptographic hashes, never as plain text.

No system is perfectly secure. If you become aware of a security issue in the app, please contact us immediately.

12. Changes to This Policy

If we make significant changes to this policy, we will update the effective date at the top and notify users through the app where reasonably practicable. Continued use of the app after a change is posted means you accept the updated policy.
